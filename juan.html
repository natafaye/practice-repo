The Bean, The Cup, The Ritual

Coffee is not a beverage. It is a practice — the careful attention paid each morning to water temperature, grind size, and the slow bloom of dark grounds meeting heat. We source exclusively from farms we visit, working with growers in Ethiopia, Colombia, and Sumatra.

Every bag is roasted in-house within 48 hours of your order. Freshness is not a selling point; it is a philosophy.

"Good coffee doesn't need anything added to it. It just needs someone paying attention."

How to Brew Well

Pour Over

Use 15g of medium-coarse ground coffee per 250ml of water at 93°C. Pour 50ml first and wait 45 seconds for the bloom. Add water in slow spirals over three minutes total. Patience is the only technique that matters.

French Press

Coarse grind, 1:15 ratio. Pour all water at once, stir gently, place the lid on without pressing, and wait exactly four minutes. Press slowly. Serve immediately — never let it sit.

Espresso

Fine grind, 18g in, 36g out, 27–30 seconds. Dial in by taste: too sour means grind finer or pull longer; too bitter means grind coarser or pull shorter. There are no shortcuts, only practice.
